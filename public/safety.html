SAFETY & HEALTH

The safety and health of the employees are part of the priorities of DASH.

The company provided all necessary measures to guarantee its people of a safe workplace that they deserve.


2010 Fire Drill

In its objectives to provide an orderly emergency response plan for all the occupants; to ensure fast, organized and smooth evacuation of the building during emergencies; and to train fire drill and emergency evacuation officers to conduct their duties successfully, the Emergency Response conducted a Fire Drill last March 26, 2010.
DASH Fun Run in 2009

Dash Engineering Philippines Incorporated (DASH) in coordination with the Occupational Safety and Health Committee (OSHC) conducted a company wide "Fun Run" last May 9, 2009 at the Cebu City Sports Complex. The employees and Japanese supervisors led by company Vice-President, Mr. Harushige Kuriyama, joined the said activity aimed to promote health awareness, strengthened comradeship and a stress-free lifestyle.

Warm-up exercises opened the activity followed by a thirty-minute run. The activity featured parlor games – flag relay and tug-of war and ended with the awarding of the winners of these games.
First Aid Training 2009

Twenty one First Aiders of the Emergency Response Team attended a Basic First Aid, Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) and Automated External Defibrillator (AED) Training last June 10-11, 2009 at DASH Engineering Philippines Incorporated (DASH) Training Room.

The two-day activity was handled by Mr. Jonathan Phillip Obregon and Mr. Jeremiah Jay Marloue Origenes, certified trainers from Basic Lifesaving Solutions – Training Center (BLS-TC).

DASH members attend Basic Occupational Safety and Health (BOSH) Training

DASH Engineering Philippines Incorporated (DASH) participated in the Department of Labor and Employment Bureau of Working Conditions (DOLE-BWC) prescribed 40-hour Basic Occupational Safety and Health (BOSH) Training last September 8 - 12, 2008 at the Five Star Training Facility, V. Rama Avenue, Calamba, Cebu City.

Selected core members of DASH Occupational Safety and Health Committee (DASH OSHC) attended the training.
